  By Yogesh Mehta, VP, Equity Advisory     The Nifty opened marginally positive at 8,583, hit a high of 8,585, declined slowly to 8,504 during the day in the last half-an-hour and closed at 8,509, down 57 points or 0.66 per cent. The MidCap100 ended 0.50 per cent lower at 14,297, while the Small Cap100 came down 0.4 per cent to 5,993. India VIX ended at 15.99.     The overall market was divided 38:62 between advances and declines. As many as 29 stocks hit new 52-week highs. Among the stocks that hit lifetime highs were ACC, Ambuja Cement, Bajaj Auto, Grasim, HDFC Bank, Hind Petro, BPCL, IOC.     There was profit booking on the PSU bank counters, which made Nifty Bank to lose 1.56 per cent, while realty, auto, pharma, financial services indices lost between 1 per cent and 1.50 per cent.     Among the gainers, HPCL (post bonus announcement) and ACC were up by 4 per cent and Ultratech 2 per cent while Ashok Leyland gained 4 per cent on strong financial results. Among the losers, Axis Bank was down 4 per cent ahead of Friday's earnings. Tata Steel, Hindalco and Vedanta slipped 2 per cent each.     The market opened stable, traded choppy but slid gradually in the second half after Kotak Bank's earnings triggered profit booking. PSU bank stocks lost between 3 per cent and 5 per cent while metal stocks fell 2 per cent.     Technically, on weekly charts of Nifty have strong resistance in the 8,600-8,650 band. Any decisive move and close above this can fuel further upside till 8,750. In case the Nifty does not hold above this level, a correction is possible till the 8,380 level.     On Friday, the market will watch out for the earning numbers from Axis Bank, Federal Bank, M&M; Finance and Persistent Systems.

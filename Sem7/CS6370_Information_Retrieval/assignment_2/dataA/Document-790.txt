Title : Sensex reclaims mount 28K; 4 factors fuelling rally on D-Street - The Economic Times

Date :  Aug 12, 2016, 04.51 PM IST

Content:
  NEW DELHI: The S&P; BSE Sensex closed 292 points higher on Friday to reclaim its crucial psychological level of 28000. The rally in the index was led by gains in HDFC, RIL, ITC, Axis Bank, Tata Motors, and L&T.;     The Nifty50 surged above its crucial level of 8,650 comfortably supported by gains in realty, power, oil & gas, capital goods, and banking stocks.     We have collated list if top 4 factors which could be fuelling rally on D-Street:    SBI Q1 results bucks trend  : The S&P; SBE Sensex picked up momentum after India's largest public sector bank reported results for the quarter ended June 30 which was higher than ETNow estimates.     SBI was also the top gainer among the 30-index components contributing over 6o points to the index tally. State Bank of India rose over 8 per cent after the largest public sector bank reported 31.7 per cent year-on-year (YoY) drop in the standalone net profit which was higher than the ETNow estimate of Rs 2,475 crore.    Global markets @record high:  Indian market started on a muted note but gained momentum in morning trade supported by positive global cues.     All three major U.S. stock indexes closed at record highs on Thursday for the first time since 1999. It was the first time since Dec. 31, 1999, that the S&P; 500, Dow Jones industrial average and Nasdaq had closed at record highs on the same day, said a media report.    Technical Breakout:  The Nifty50 break above its crucial resistance level of 8,600-8,620 which has now opened the potential for further upside which could take the index towards 8,700 levels.     "The index has crossed above 8,620 levels and that has opened the doorway for further upside towards 8,665 and then towards 8,720 zones while on the downside support exists at 8530 then 8475 zones," Chandan Taparia, Derivatives & Technical Analyst - Equity Research at Anand Rathi Financial Services told ETMarkets.com.    Oil prices rise:  Markets across the globe rallied after Brent Crude oil rallied more than 4 per cent on Thursday. Brent crude futures were trading at $46.12 per barrel.     "Markets were pushed up as Saudi Arabia's energy minister Khalid al-Falih said late on Thursday that oil producers would discuss potential action to stabilize oil prices during a meeting next month in Algeria," said a media report.
